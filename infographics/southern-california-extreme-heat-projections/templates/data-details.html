Forecasting Southern California's extreme heat days

Produced by Sanden Totten and Chris Keller | Published Sept. 9, 2015

The animation below shows average number of extremely hot days per year (defined as days hotter than 95 degrees Fahrenheit) between 1981 and 2000 (orange bars) and projected for the years between 2041 and 2060 (blue bars), based on UCLA professor Alex Hall's Climate Change in the Los Angeles Region Project.

The future projection is based on data from more than 30 global climate models brought to neighborhood-scale resolution, and it represents a "business as usual" scenario assuming greenhouse gas emissions continue to increase.

Source(s): UCLA Center for Climate Change Solutions. Built using Quartz's Chartbuilder, underscore.js, backbone.js, highcharts.js & Bootstrap.
