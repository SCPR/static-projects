Volunteer Opportunities in Southern California

Season's Givings

This season, we asked KPCC listeners where they volunteer during the holidays, and what opportunities our audience should know about. Hear volunteers talk about the work they do and why they do it, explore our listing of charitable organizations that could use your help.

Use the database below to search for volunteer and charitable opportunities this holiday season. Type in any term you choose — location, type of donations needed, clients served, etc. Are there organizations we missed? Let us know! Or share your story of holiday giving!

The 2012 Mayoral race

The Jewish Home in Reseda welcomes volunteers

Thirty elderly men and women face the cafeteria entrance, waiting. Some are in hospice care or rehab therapy; others have Alzheimer’s. Their average age is about 90. After 15 minutes, kids show up – some as young as four. They’re with their moms, and they’re carrying trays of cookies imported from Israel and blue balloons that say “Happy Hanukkah.”

Volunteer to help 'SPARK' a young apprentice's focus

When kids get to middle school, they start asking their teachers: Why am I learning this? What’s the point? For two years in the Los Angeles area, the SPARK program has responded to those questions through apprenticeships for low-income middle school students who are struggling in school. The program needs more volunteer mentors for its young apprentices.

'Project Angel Food' volunteers prepare and deliver meals to the chronically ill

Every weekday, between the hours of 9 and 11:00 a.m., the kitchen at Project Angel Food is bustling. Pots clang, trays are stacked and unstacked, carts are wheeled, ovens slam shut, knives slice on cutting boards, staff cooks and volunteers alike yell, laugh, and sing their way through the morning's preparations.

South LA kids make PALs with LAPD

The makeshift training area underneath the LAPD's Newton Division isn't much. That's because it's also the police station's underground parking garage. Among the police cruisers, there's a big mat, a couple of punching bags and assorted strength-and-conditioning equipment.

Sherman Oaks nonprofit 'The Help Group' provides support for children with special needs

Based in Sherman Oaks, The Help Group operates nine day schools on seven campuses in the Los Angeles area to provide services to children with autism, learning disabilities, ADHD, developmental delays, abuse and emotional problems.

Santa Ana group needs volunteers to help with 'motel children' party

On many city streets, it’s hard not to see homeless people struggling to get by, but at Grandma’s House of Hope, founder Je’net Kreitneris says the goal is to help those people we don’t see. Kreitneris founded the Santa Ana-based organization in 2004 as a way to give back to the community that lifted her and her young son out of homelessness.

Volunteers needed to perform Los Angeles County civil wedding ceremonies

As engaged couples pack into Los Angeles County Registrar-Recorder offices to tie the knot on the last triple date of the century -- 12-12-12 -- they might not realize that the officiant wearing the black robe and reciting the ceremony is a volunteer.

Torrance Memorial Medical Center's volunteer clowns bring cheer to patients

Brandy Moss-Scott was putting up her Christmas tree, when she twisted her foot, broke her leg, tore a ligament and shattered her ankle in three places. Now, she’s stuck in a hospital bed at Torrance Memorial Medical Center. She passed the time on Monday by watching TV in her empty hospital room, when she received some unexpected visitors—volunteer clowns Joy and Lady Bug.

Downtown Women's Center serves as 'safe haven' for thousands of Skid Row homeless

The Downtown Women’s Center (DWC) has been serving homeless women on Skid Row since 1978 -- providing warm meals and hot showers, medical services, computer labs, educational opportunities and long-term housing options.

Marilyn Hoyt at The Pasadena Humane Society

Each year the Pasadena Humane Society provides shelter and care for a number of abandoned or homeless animals. In 2011, the organization took in nearly 12,000 animals found in La Canada Flintridge, San Marino, Sierra Madre, Arcadia, Glendale, Pasadena and South Pasadena. In 2011, the PHS took in 11,929 animals.
