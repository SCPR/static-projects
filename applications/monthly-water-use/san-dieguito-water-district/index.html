September monthly water use by the San Dieguito Water District

Water consumption in the San Dieguito Water District increased 0.98 percent in September 2016 compared to September 2015. Overall, consumption in September has decreased 20.83 percent over the past four years.
  • September 2013 212 million gallons
  • September 2014 205 million gallons
  • September 2015 154 million gallons
  • September 2016 155 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 85.0% of water consumption in the San Dieguito Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 1.68 percent in September 2016 compared to September 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

San Dieguito Water District water use decreased by 20.85 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
