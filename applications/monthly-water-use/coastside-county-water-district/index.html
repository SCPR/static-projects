July monthly water use by the Coastside County Water District

Water consumption in the Coastside County Water District increased 18.66 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016. Overall, consumption in July has decreased 3.89 percent over the past five years.
  • July 2013 76 million gallons
  • July 2014 75 million gallons
  • July 2015 57 million gallons
  • July 2016 61 million gallons
  • July 2017 73 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 32.0% of water consumption in the Coastside County Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 5.25 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Coastside County Water District water use decreased by 21.59 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 8 percent reduction.
9.863
billion gallons consumed
over the same months in 2013.
7.734

between June 2015 and February 2016.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
