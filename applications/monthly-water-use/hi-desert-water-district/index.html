March monthly water use by the Hi-Desert Water District

Water consumption in the Hi-Desert Water District decreased 5.82 percent in March 2017 compared to March 2016. Overall, consumption in March has decreased 10.21 percent over the past four years.

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 71.42% of water consumption in the Hi-Desert Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 3.23 percent in March 2017 compared to March 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Hi-Desert Water District water use decreased by 8.15 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 16 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
