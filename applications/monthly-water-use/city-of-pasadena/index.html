July monthly water use by the City Of Pasadena

Water consumption in the City Of Pasadena increased 9.05 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016. Overall, consumption in July has decreased 9.91 percent over the past five years.
  • July 2013 1133 million gallons
  • July 2014 1090 million gallons

  • July 2016 936 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 66.3% of water consumption in the City Of Pasadena is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 11.02 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

City Of Pasadena water use decreased by 23.16 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.
142.145
billion gallons consumed
over the same months in 2013.
109.220
billion gallons consumed

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
