January monthly water use by the South Coast Water District

Water consumption in the South Coast Water District decreased 6.36 percent in January 2017 compared to January 2016. Overall, consumption in January has decreased 22.70 percent over the past four years.

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 76.7% of water consumption in the South Coast Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 1.48 percent in January 2017 compared to January 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

South Coast Water District water use decreased by 26.78 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 24 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
