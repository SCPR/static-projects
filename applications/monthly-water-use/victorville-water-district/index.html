January monthly water use by the Victorville Water District

Water consumption in the Victorville Water District increased 16.91 percent in January 2017 compared to January 2016. Overall, consumption in January has decreased 10.85 percent over the past four years.

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 60.0% of water consumption in the Victorville Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 8.77 percent in January 2017 compared to January 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Victorville Water District water use decreased by 25.04 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
