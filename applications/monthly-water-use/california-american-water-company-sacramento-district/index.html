June monthly water use by the California-American Water Company Sacramento District

Water consumption in the California-American Water Company Sacramento District increased 15.62 percent in June 2016 compared to June 2015. Overall, consumption in June has decreased 28.48 percent over the past four years.
  • June 2013 1186 million gallons
  • June 2014 1132 million gallons
  • June 2015 826 million gallons
  • June 2016 955 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 77.0% of water consumption in the California-American Water Company Sacramento District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 14.14 percent in June 2016 compared to June 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

California-American Water Company Sacramento District water use decreased by 35.75 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 20 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
