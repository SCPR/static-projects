The water use reduction target — approved by the State Water Resources Control Board this year in order to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide — is based on a June 11 revision tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of 184.20 gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers of reduction targets.

Average daily water use of each residential customer

The State Water Resources Control Board also collects a figure that represents the average daily water use of each residential customer for a given water agency. Comparing Aug. 2015 — the latest monthly data available — to July 2015, average daily per capita residential water use for the Cucamonga Valley Water District increased.

Residential customers in the Cucamonga Valley Water District used 36.63% more water in Aug. 2015 when compared to the statewide average daily per capita residential water use of 101.66 gallons.
Here's a view of average daily per capita residential water use over the past 15 months. Water use by this agency's residential customers has increased by 6.19%, going from an average of 130.8 gallons used by each resident per day to 138.9.
