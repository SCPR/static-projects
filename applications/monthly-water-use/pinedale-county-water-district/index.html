December monthly water use by the Pinedale County Water District

Water consumption in the Pinedale County Water District increased 7.01 percent in December 2016 compared to December 2015. Overall, consumption in December has decreased 20.12 percent over the past four years.
  • December 2013 48 million gallons
  • December 2014 37 million gallons
  • December 2015 36 million gallons
  • December 2016 38 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 94.04% of water consumption in the Pinedale County Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 7.01 percent in December 2016 compared to December 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Pinedale County Water District water use decreased by 29.75 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 8 percent reduction.
5.437

over the same months in 2013.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
