June monthly water use by the Walnut Valley Water District

Water consumption in the Walnut Valley Water District increased 7.60 percent in June 2017 compared to June 2016. Overall, consumption in June has decreased 16.82 percent over the past five years.
  • June 2013 678 million gallons
  • June 2014 657 million gallons

  • June 2016 524 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 85.0% of water consumption in the Walnut Valley Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 6.35 percent in June 2017 compared to June 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Walnut Valley Water District water use decreased by 24.63 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.
88.152
billion gallons consumed
over the same months in 2013.
66.436
billion gallons consumed
between June 2015 and February 2016.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
