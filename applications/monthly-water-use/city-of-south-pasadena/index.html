July monthly water use by the City Of South Pasadena

Water consumption in the City Of South Pasadena increased 10.70 percent in July 2016 compared to July 2015. Overall, consumption in July has decreased 24.78 percent over the past four years.
  • July 2013 149 million gallons
  • July 2014 139 million gallons
  • July 2015 101 million gallons
  • July 2016 112 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 71.0% of water consumption in the City Of South Pasadena is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 7.42 percent in July 2016 compared to July 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

City Of South Pasadena water use decreased by 27.79 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
