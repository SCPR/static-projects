September monthly water use by the Golden State Water Company Bell-Bell Gardens

Water consumption in the Golden State Water Company Bell-Bell Gardens increased 1.11 percent in September 2016 compared to September 2015. Overall, consumption in September has decreased 15.54 percent over the past four years.
  • September 2013 152 million gallons
  • September 2014 141 million gallons
  • September 2015 127 million gallons
  • September 2016 128 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 75.0% of water consumption in the Golden State Water Company Bell-Bell Gardens is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 1.08 percent in September 2016 compared to September 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Golden State Water Company Bell-Bell Gardens water use decreased by 13.45 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 8 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
