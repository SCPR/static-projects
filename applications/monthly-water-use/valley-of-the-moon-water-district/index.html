October monthly water use by the Valley Of The Moon Water District

Water consumption in the Valley Of The Moon Water District decreased 11.13 percent in October 2016 compared to October 2015. Overall, consumption in October has decreased 26.79 percent over the past four years.
  • October 2013 105 million gallons
  • October 2014 75 million gallons
  • October 2015 87 million gallons
  • October 2016 77 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 81.9% of water consumption in the Valley Of The Moon Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 1.17 percent in October 2016 compared to October 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Valley Of The Moon Water District water use decreased by 30.08 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 20 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
