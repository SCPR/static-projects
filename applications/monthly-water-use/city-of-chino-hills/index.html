June monthly water use by the City Of Chino Hills

Water consumption in the City Of Chino Hills increased 3.96 percent in June 2016 compared to June 2015. Overall, consumption in June has decreased 19.52 percent over the past four years.
  • June 2013 509 million gallons
  • June 2014 489 million gallons
  • June 2015 394 million gallons
  • June 2016 410 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 76.0% of water consumption in the City Of Chino Hills is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 5.35 percent in June 2016 compared to June 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

City Of Chino Hills water use decreased by 26.31 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
