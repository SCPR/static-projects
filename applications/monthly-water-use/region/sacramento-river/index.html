About the data: These charts show the daily per capita residential water use rate in California as calculated by the State Water Resources Control Board. The charts cover the Sept. 2015 reporting period, and are compared to the previous month unless specified. Since the per capita measure (which reflects gallons of water used per resident per day) does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.

Sacramento River

Daily per capita residential water use decreased in Sept. 2015 to an average of 140.88 gallons for the 41 agencies in the Sacramento River hydrologic region. That number is compared to the Aug. 2015 daily per capita residential water use of 149.94. In order to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide, the State Water Resources Control Board approved water use reduction targets for each urban water agency in the state.

The table below shows the 41 agencies in the Sacramento River hydrologic region. Of these, 14 met, 23 failed to meet and 4 came within 95 percent of their reduction targets.

Progress toward meeting the reduction targets are based on comparing residential water consumption between June 1, 2015 and Feb. 29, 2016 to the same time period beginning in 2013. The percent change is calculated between current use and the 2013 baseline and compared to the reduction target.
