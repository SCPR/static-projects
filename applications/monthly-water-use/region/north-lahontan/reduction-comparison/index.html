Comparing cutbacks called for in state water conservation proposals

Produced by Molly Peterson & Chris Keller | Published April 17, 2015

After an outcry from water agencies, the State Water Resources Control Board has updated its plan to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use, shrinking required cuts on some communities while demanding even larger cuts from the heaviest users.

The amount of water cities must conserve depends on their average per-capita water use from July through September of last year. The original plan only considered September use.

Previously, cities were divided into reduction tiers of 10, 20, 25 and 35 percent. The new plan envisions nine tiers ranging from 8 to 36 percent.

This chart compares the latest proposed cuts to water use for North Lahontan water agencies with the first draft. Several agencies have a lower threshold to meet under the new framework. The chart also compares an agency's three-month daily per capita residential water use rate—from July to Sept. 2014—used in the new plan with the original one-month rate—from Sept. 2014.

About the data: These charts show the daily per capita residential water use rate in California as calculated by the State Water Resources Control Board. The charts cover the Feb. 2015 reporting period, and compared to the baseline measure from the same month in 2013. Since the per capita measure (which reflects gallons of water used per resident per day) does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.
