About the data: These charts show the daily per capita residential water use rate in California as calculated by the State Water Resources Control Board. The charts cover the Dec. 2015 reporting period, and are compared to the previous month unless specified. Since the per capita measure (which reflects gallons of water used per resident per day) does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.

San Francisco Bay

Daily per capita residential water use decreased in Dec. 2015 to an average of 51.12 gallons for the 46 agencies in the San Francisco Bay hydrologic region. That number is compared to the Nov. 2015 daily per capita residential water use of 55.09. Progress toward meeting the reduction targets are based on comparing residential water consumption between June 1, 2015 and Feb. 29, 2016 to the same time period beginning in 2013. The percent change is calculated between current use and the 2013 baseline and compared to the reduction target.
