About the data: These charts show the daily per capita residential water use rate in California as calculated by the State Water Resources Control Board. The charts cover the Nov. 2015 reporting period, and are compared to the previous month unless specified. Since the per capita measure (which reflects gallons of water used per resident per day) does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.

South Coast

Daily per capita residential water use decreased in Nov. 2015 to an average of 77.59 gallons for the 176 agencies in the South Coast hydrologic region. That number is compared to the Oct. 2015 daily per capita residential water use of 82.19. Progress toward meeting the reduction targets are based on comparing residential water consumption between June 1, 2015 and Feb. 29, 2016 to the same time period beginning in 2013. The percent change is calculated between current use and the 2013 baseline and compared to the reduction target.
