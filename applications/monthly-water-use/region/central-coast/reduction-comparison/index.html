Comparing Central Coast cutbacks called for in state water conservation proposals

Produced by Molly Peterson & Chris Keller | Updated July 2, 2015

After an outcry from water agencies, the State Water Resources Control Board has updated its plan to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use, shrinking required cuts on some communities while demanding even larger cuts from the heaviest users.

The amount of water cities must conserve depends on their average per-capita water use from July through September of last year. The original plan only considered September use.

Previously, cities were divided into reduction tiers of 10, 20, 25 and 35 percent. The new plan envisions nine tiers ranging from 8 to 36 percent.

About the data: This chart compares the three proposals to require Central Coast water agencies to cut their water production. Several agencies have a lower threshold to meet under the new framework. Others have a higher threshold. The chart also compares an agency's three-month daily per capita residential water use rate—from July to Sept. 2014—used in the updated plans with the original one-month rate—from Sept. 2014. Although approved, the State Water Resources Control Board said water agencies will have until May 31 to submit verfied data that could affect placement in this tier system. Since the per capita measure (which reflects gallons of water used per resident per day) does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.
