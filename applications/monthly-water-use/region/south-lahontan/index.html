About the data: These charts show the daily per capita residential water use rate in California as calculated by the State Water Resources Control Board. The charts cover the August 2016 reporting period, and are compared to the previous month unless specified. Since the per capita measure (which reflects gallons of water used per resident per day) does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.

South Lahontan

Daily per capita residential water use decreased in August 2016 to an average of 149.11 gallons for the 17 agencies in the South Lahontan hydrologic region. That number is compared to the July 2016 daily per capita residential water use of 160.93.
