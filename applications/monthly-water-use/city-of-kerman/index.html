July monthly water use by the City Of Kerman

Water consumption in the City Of Kerman increased 2.96 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016. Overall, consumption in July has decreased 18.11 percent over the past five years.
  • July 2013 150 million gallons
  • July 2014 138 million gallons
  • July 2015 104 million gallons
  • July 2016 120 million gallons
  • July 2017 123 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 70.0% of water consumption in the City Of Kerman is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 2.96 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

City Of Kerman water use decreased by 27.80 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 32 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
