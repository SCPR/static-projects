August monthly water use by the City Of Monrovia

Water consumption in the City Of Monrovia decreased 8.60 percent in August 2017 compared to August 2016. Overall, consumption in August has decreased 25.08 percent over the past five years.
  • August 2013 180 million gallons
  • August 2014 136 million gallons

  • August 2016 133 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 74.0% of water consumption in the City Of Monrovia is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 20.39 percent in August 2017 compared to August 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

City Of Monrovia water use decreased by 22.37 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
