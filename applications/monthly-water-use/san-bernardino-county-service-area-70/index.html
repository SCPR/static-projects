December monthly water use by the San Bernardino County Service Area 70

Water consumption in the San Bernardino County Service Area 70 increased 5.75 percent in December 2016 compared to December 2015. Overall, consumption in December has decreased 0.47 percent over the past four years.
  • December 2013 29 million gallons
  • December 2014 26 million gallons
  • December 2015 27 million gallons
  • December 2016 29 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 33.1% of water consumption in the San Bernardino County Service Area 70 is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 59.07 percent in December 2016 compared to December 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

San Bernardino County Service Area 70 water use decreased by 24.60 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.

About the data: This data is for urban water suppliers and is based on the the most recent reports submitted to the State Water Resources Control Board. Since the gallons of water used per person per day measurement does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.
