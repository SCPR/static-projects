June monthly water use by the City Of Orange

Water consumption in the City Of Orange increased 14.45 percent in June 2017 compared to June 2016. Overall, consumption in June has decreased 11.45 percent over the past five years.
  • June 2013 972 million gallons
  • June 2014 978 million gallons
  • June 2015 743 million gallons
  • June 2016 752 million gallons
  • June 2017 861 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 60.7% of water consumption in the City Of Orange is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 12.64 percent in June 2017 compared to June 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

City Of Orange water use decreased by 26.31 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 28 percent reduction.
123.959
billion gallons consumed
over the same months in 2013.
91.350

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
