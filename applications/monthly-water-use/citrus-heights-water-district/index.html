July monthly water use by the Citrus Heights Water District

Water consumption in the Citrus Heights Water District increased 6.86 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016. Overall, consumption in July has decreased 19.82 percent over the past five years.
  • July 2013 671 million gallons
  • July 2014 529 million gallons
  • July 2015 386 million gallons
  • July 2016 503 million gallons
  • July 2017 538 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 95.0% of water consumption in the Citrus Heights Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 6.86 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Citrus Heights Water District water use decreased by 34.94 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 32 percent reduction.
62.944

over the same months in 2013.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
