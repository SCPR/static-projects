August monthly water use by the Sacramento Suburban Water District

Water consumption in the Sacramento Suburban Water District increased 25.40 percent in August 2016 compared to August 2015. Overall, consumption in August has decreased 14.20 percent over the past four years.
  • August 2013 1545 million gallons
  • August 2014 1241 million gallons
  • August 2015 1057 million gallons
  • August 2016 1326 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 67.0% of water consumption in the Sacramento Suburban Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer increased 15.72 percent in August 2016 compared to August 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Sacramento Suburban Water District water use decreased by 30.68 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 32 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
