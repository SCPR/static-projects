August monthly water use by the City Of San Diego

Water consumption in the City Of San Diego decreased 6.30 percent in August 2017 compared to August 2016. Overall, consumption in August has decreased 20.80 percent over the past five years.

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 54.0% of water consumption in the City Of San Diego is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 8.06 percent in August 2017 compared to August 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

City Of San Diego water use decreased by 18.40 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 16 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.

Since the gallons of water used per person per day measurement does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.
