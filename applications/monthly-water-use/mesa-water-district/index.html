July monthly water use by the Mesa Water District

Water consumption in the Mesa Water District increased 3.73 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016. Overall, consumption in July has decreased 6.93 percent over the past five years.
  • July 2013 585 million gallons
  • July 2014 600 million gallons
  • July 2015 434 million gallons
  • July 2016 514 million gallons
  • July 2017 533 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 59.7% of water consumption in the Mesa Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 33.84 percent in July 2017 compared to July 2016, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Mesa Water District water use decreased by 22.32 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — achieving a 20 percent reduction.
77.136
billion gallons consumed
over the same months in 2013.
59.922

between June 2015 and February 2016.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate that placed water agencies into nine tiers.
