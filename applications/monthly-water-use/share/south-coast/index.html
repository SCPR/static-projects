About the data: These charts show the daily per capita residential water use rate in California as calculated by the State Water Resources Control Board. The charts cover the June 2016 reporting period, and are compared to the previous month unless specified. Since the per capita measure (which reflects gallons of water used per resident per day) does not take into account rainfall, temperature, humidity and population density, the State Water Resources Control Board discourages comparisons between water suppliers.

South Coast

Daily per capita residential water use increased in June 2016 to an average of 92.97 gallons for the 172 agencies in the South Coast hydrologic region. That number is compared to the May 2016 daily per capita residential water use of 80.42.
