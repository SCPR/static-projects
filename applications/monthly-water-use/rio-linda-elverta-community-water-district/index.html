November monthly water use by the Rio Linda - Elverta Community Water District

Water consumption in the Rio Linda - Elverta Community Water District decreased 4.36 percent in November 2016 compared to November 2015. Overall, consumption in November has decreased 37.04 percent over the past four years.
  • November 2013 58 million gallons
  • November 2014 42 million gallons
  • November 2015 38 million gallons
  • November 2016 36 million gallons

Average daily water consumption by each residential customer

About 86.3% of water consumption in the Rio Linda - Elverta Community Water District is residential. The average daily gallons used by each residential customer decreased 3.88 percent in November 2016 compared to November 2015, according to the State Water Resources Control Board.

Progress toward mandatory water reduction*

Rio Linda - Elverta Community Water District water use decreased by 33.11 percent between June 2015 and February 2016 — the nine months of the initial statewide conservation mandate — but the agency failed to meet a 36 percent reduction.

*: Approved by the State Water Resources Control Board in 2015, the reduction target was meant to achieve a 25 percent reduction in water use statewide. It is tied to an average July through Sept. 2014 daily per capita residential water use rate of gallons that places water agencies into nine tiers.
