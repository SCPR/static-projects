LAUSD school by school budgets

Produced by Annie Gilbertson, Claire Withycombe & Chris Keller | Updated June 18, 2014

The Los Angeles Unified School District gets $6.8 billion to educate its 650,000 students, plus another $5.2 billion to build and maintain schools, provide adult education and service debts.

This data set shows how administrators plan to spend the $4.6 billion headed directly to schools next year. But their choices are few: the biggest chunk of money will be doled out to teacher salaries and pension obligations, which put many schools in the red.
