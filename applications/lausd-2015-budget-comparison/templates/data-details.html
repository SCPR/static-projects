L.A. Unified budgets by school

Produced by Annie Gilbertson & Chris Keller | Updated June 14, 2014

The Los Angeles Unified School District gets $6.8 billion from the state to educate its 650,000 students. It receives about another $1 billion from the federal government.

This data set shows how administrators plan to spend $4.6 billion in the 2014-15 school year. But their choices are few: the biggest chunk of money will be dolled out to teacher salaries and pension obligations, which put many schools in the red.

Officials propose spending more than $3 billion for central management and district-wide initiatives, such as arts integration and classroom technology coaching.

Source(s): Los Angeles Unified School District school-by-school spending breakdown, proposed budget released June 9, 2014.
