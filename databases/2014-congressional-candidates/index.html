Candidates for Southern California congressional seats

Produced by Chris Keller & Kitty Felde | Updated March 14, 2014

This year, voters will go to the polls to select representatives for all of California's 53 congressional seats, starting with a primary on June 3 and culminating with the general election on Nov. 4.

The field of those vying for seats in Southern California districts is more clear now that Tuesday's deadline for candidates to file paperwork for the primary has passed.

Below is a list of those who have filed the necessary paperwork to run for Congressional districts in Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside and San Bernardino counties. It is based on data available Thursday from those counties.

The list may change up until the secretary of State's office releases the certified list of candidates on March 27. Until that date, counties continue to process paperwork from candidates and check eligibility.

Six incumbents – five from Southern California – chose not to run in 2014:

  • Henry Waxman from Los Angeles' 33rd District
  • George Miller (11th District)
  • Gary Miller (31st District)
  • Buck McKeon (25th District)
  • John Campbell (45th District)
  • Gloria Negrete McLeod (35th District)

When those retirements are combined with the election of 14 brand new representatives in the 2012 election, about 38 percent of the state's delegation will have less than three years of experience on the Hill by the time they take office in January 2015.

One Congressional newcomer will come from the more than 20 candidates that have filed to run for Waxman's 33rd District seat. The Los Angeles Registrar-Recorder/County Clerk's website lists 25 potential candidates for Waxman's seat. Only 21 of those are listed as having filed nomination papers.

Los Angeles Registrar-Recorder/County Clerk spokesman Elizabeth Knox said the county continues to process and review paperwork, so the list could grow to the full 25 candidates. Updates are to the list each night at 7 p.m., she said.

California allows anyone from any party to vote for any candidate in the June 3 primary, with the top two finishers facing off in November.
