California Drought

Are you concerned about the drought?

Produced by Chris Keller | Updated March 7, 2014

Last month Gov. Jerry Brown declared a drought emergency in California and asked state residents to voluntarily cut water usage by 20 percent.

And in Northern California, some counties and cities have begun mandatory conservation measures for residents. But in Southern California, most local water districts have only asked for voluntary measures.

But are those realities enough to concern you about the drought and influence your water use? Click a point on the grid below and let us know how concerned you are, and how vigilant you are about water conservation.

For instance, if you are concerned about the drought and taking serious steps to conserve water, you would click a point in the upper right and submit your thoughts. Likewise, if you are not concerned and your water use hasn't changed, click a point in the lower left of the grid.

Source(s): KPCC audience submissions. Built using code made available by the WNYC Data News Team, using highcharts.js, handlebars.js, Miso Dataset & Bootstrap.
