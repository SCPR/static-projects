Lead remediation efforts by the city of Los Angeles

Produced by Deepa Fernandes and Will Craft | Published Sept. 8, 2015

As children nationwide continued to be poisoned due to lead in and around their homes, the Housing and Urban Development Department (HUD) began a grants program to allow cities to send in contractors with expertise in properly remediating lead. In 2006 (Check this!), Los Angeles began its own program where homeowners can qualify to have their property remediated of lead if they meet certain criteria. The owner must be low-income and there must be a child aged six or under that lives at the property or regularly visits.

Liseth Romero-Martinez began managing the CIty's Lead Hazard Remediation Program (LHRP) in 2007. She created a list of all the properties that had been successfully remediated of lead. "I felt that families looking for a Lead Safe unit could utilize the Lead Registry," she said. However in recent years the list has not been publicly available.

The average cost per remediation project is approximately $7700, according to Jose Berumen, of the LHRP. "We try to stay within the cost limits," he said. "The program has justified expenditures of over $20,000 on a single project with prior HUD budget approval."

His team will assess the parts of each housing unit that have lead dangers. The family will be temporarily moved out while a team from the City conducts the work of removing windows or doors, stripping hazardous paint, and repainting.
