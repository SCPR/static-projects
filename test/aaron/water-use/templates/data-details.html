Map: Where water use is up in California this year

Produced by Aaron Mendelson | Published September 7, 2016

After months of mandatory water cutbacks in response to a historic drought, earlier this year California's Water Board asked water districts to conduct "stress tests" to determine their water needs. Most decided they had enough water to get through three more years of drought, and wouldn't require their customers to cut back. That's led to a bump in water use this summer compared to 2015, across much of the state. Explore the map to learn more.

Source: State Water Resources Control Board, California Department of Water Resources. Built using underscore.js, backbone.js, Google Fusion Tables & Bootstrap.

The GIS data here was compiled by the Department of Water Resources. Spokeswoman Lauren Bisnett said that "it comes from a wide range of sources of a wide range of qualities. There isn’t a better water agency dataset in existence to our knowledge, but ours should be considered a rough." KPCC was able to identify 385 of the districts in the monthly urban water use reports, from the State Water Resources Control Board — that's what you see below.
