Does your county turn out to vote in midterm elections?

Produced by Aaron Mendelson | Published June 5, 2018

This year's midterm elections will decide which party controls congress the and set the candidates for governor in California. Will that entice voters to head to the polls?

Probably not, if history is any guide.

Over the last seven midterm primary elections, statewide turnout hasn't topped 50 percent once. And turnout has often been worse in Los Angeles County, hitting a low of 17 percent in 2014. Midterm elections typically suffer from a lack of voter enthusiasm, without presidential candidates on the ballot. Will 2018 be different? We'll find out once the polls close.
