Truck collisions on the rise, more common in I.E.

Produced by Aaron Mendelson | Published March 31, 2017

Since the Great Recession officially ended in June 2009, collisions involving large trucks have been on the rise across Southern California. Not only have their numbers jumped, but the share of collisions that involved big trucks has increased. That's significant, as truck collisions are twice as likely to be fatal.

Southern California's truck collisions are especially high in the Inland Empire counties of Riverside and San Bernardino. In 2014 — the last year for which data is available — 8.23 percent of collisions in San Berardino involved a big truck. That's double the figure for Orange County, and likely an unintended consequence of the boom in warehousing in the area.
