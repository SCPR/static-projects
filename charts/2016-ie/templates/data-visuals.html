Who's funding the charter push?

A small group of wealthy charter school advocates put serious money into these groups. Below are people who have given more than $500,000 to the EdVoice and the California Charter Schools Association Advocates committees since 2014 (the latter group funds related Parent Teacher Alliance).

The list includes familiar names, including Gap founder Doris Fisher, Netflix CEO Reed Hastings and members of the Walton family, who own Walmart. Many live outside the districts where their money is being spent, and some, like ex-New York mayor Michael Bloomberg and former Enron trader John Arnold live outside the state.

These donations became a campaign this issue in 2016, as the California Teachers Association sent out a mailer claiming billionaires were "playing politics" and that the Parent Teacher Alliance is "deceptively named".

The state's most expensive battle is for the 14th Assembly District, which has seen more outside spending than any race in California history. That contest pits Tim Grayson against fellow Democrat Mae Torlakson. If Torlakson's name sounds familiar, that's because she's married to State Superintenddent of Education Tom Torlakson. Charter groups spent millions trying to block Tom Torlakson's re-election in 2014, and this year EdVoice has spent about more than $3 million trying to keep Mae Torlakson out of the open seat.

Other expensive races have seen waves of spending by charter-allied groups, including the 3rd Senate District, and 27th and 43rd Assembly District races.
