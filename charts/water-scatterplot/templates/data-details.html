Drought

Income and water use in Southern California

Produced by Aaron Mendelson | Published May 7, 2015

California is in the fourth year of a historic drought, with no end in sight. This week, the State Water Resources Control Board approved the first-ever mandatory restrictions on local water agencies.

Many eyes have turned to the manicured lawns, swimming pools and fountains in wealthy communities, with the assumpiton that wealthy Californians use more than their share of the state's water. So do they? KPCC analyzed the per-capita daily water usage and household incomes for more than 40 Southern California communities; the results are below.
