Garcetti's behested payments

Produced by Aaron Mendelson | Published August 23, 2017

While Los Angeles has tight controls on the amount of money citizens can donate to campaigns, there's no limit on the amounts of cash people, foundations, nonprofits and corporations can donate to government and charity at a politician's behest.

Since Los Angeles Mayor Eric Garcetti was elected in 2013, he has reported $31.9 million in payments at his behest. The majority of those payments have gone to The Mayor's Fund For Los Angeles, a nonprofit dedicated to promoting civic causes including reducing homelessness, workforce development and gang reduction. Source: Los Angeles City Ethics Commission. Law only requires payments totaling greater than $5,000 in a single year by a donor to be reported by politicians.
