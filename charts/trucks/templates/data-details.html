Collisions on the rise, frequent in I.E.

Produced by Aaron Mendelson | Published March 31, 2017

As the economy has picked up since 2009, more trucks have been on the roads. That's meant more big truck collisions, which have an outsized cost, both financially and in terms of human life. Not only have their numbers jumped, but the share of all collisions that involve a big truck has been on the rise.

Southern California's truck collisions are especially high in the Inland Empire counties of Riverside and San Bernardino. In 2014 — the last full year for which data is available — 8.23 percent of collisions in San Bernardino involved a big truck. That's double the figure for Orange County, and connected to the boom in warehousing in the Inland Empire.
