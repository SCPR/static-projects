El Niño

How does this year's rainfall stack up?

Produced by Aaron Mendelson | Published January 25, 2015

How much rain has El Niño brought this year? It can be a tough question to answer, which is why KPCC has partnered with the Scripps Institute of Climatology to put this "monster" weather event in context.

The graph below reflects weighted rainfall data from 20 rain gauges in greater Southern California. These gauges are geographically diverse, and have detailed records strething back decades. They help give a clearer picture of how much rain is fallilng across the region this year: that's the orange line.

The gray line shows the median, or typical year. The blue line displays the median for five previous strong El Niños: 1958, 1966, 1973, 1983 and 1998. The graph shows the six-month period of the "water year" when California receives most of its rain.

Sources: Data via Dave Pierce of the Scripps Institution of Oceanography at UC San Deigo, California-Nevada Climate Applications Program and Center for Western Weather and Water Extremes. The raw data is available at this link, and is updated daily.
