Do I live in a California flood zone?

Produced by Aaron Mendelson and Chris Keller | Updated Dec. 11, 2015

A strong El Ni√±o climate pattern is on its way to Southern California, and might bring with it some much-needed rain. But that precipitation could be a double-edged sword, raising the possibility of flooding in a region struggling with drought.

The Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) tracks areas vulnerable to a 100-year flood, or a flood that has a one percent chance of occurring in any given year. FEMA requires homeowners to purchase flood insurance in those flood zones. The agency's data shows that populated areas around the state are at risk. A much broader swath of the state is vulnerable in a 500-year flood, but insurance isn't required in those areas. For more about flood mapping and insurance, and for an official determination on if you are in a flood zone, consult FEMA.

Source: Federal Emergency Management Agency's National Flood Hazard Layer for California.
