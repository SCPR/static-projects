LA's missing bus shelters

Produced by Meghan McCarty & Aaron Mendelson | Published May 2, 2016

Bus shelters protect transit users from the elements, from the summer sun to winter rains. Since the 1980’s the city of L.A. has paid for shelters by contracting with a company that sells advertising on them.

Under a 2001 contract, the company CBS/Decaux agreed to install more than 1,000 bus shelters in the city of L.A. over two decades, in exchange for a share of the advertising revenue. But the agreement has unraveled and after nearly 15 years, the majority of those shelters still haven’t gone up.

The city has issued permits for shelters at 1,450 locations but only 621 have been installed. Some areas have gotten a greater portion of their intended shelters.
