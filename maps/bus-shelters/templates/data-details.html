Map: Where LA's bus shelters have—and haven't—been installed

Produced by Meghan McCarty & Aaron Mendelson | Published May 2, 2016

Bus shelters protect transit users from the elements, from the summer sun to the winter rains. Under a 2001 agreement, a contractor promised to install hundreds of shelters in the City of Los Angeles over a two-decade period. But after nearly 15 years, a majority of those shelters still haven't gone up.

Some areas of the city have seen more bus shelters installed than others. Below, areas of Los Angeles are split up by zip code. Darker areas have seen a higher share of their shelters installed. Click on an area to see details.

Source: Los Angeles Bureau of Street Services, Zip Code Tabulation Areas (ZCTAs) from United States Census Bureau. Only ZCTAs with at least 5 planned shelters are shown here. All ZCTAs include some part of Los Angeles. Built using underscore.js, backbone.js, Google Fusion Tables & Bootstrap.
