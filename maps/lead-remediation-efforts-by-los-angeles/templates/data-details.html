Lead remediation efforts by the city of Los Angeles

Produced by Deepa Fernandes and Will Craft | Published Sept. 8, 2015

As children nationwide continue to be found with lead poisoning brought on by sources in and around their homes, the U.S. Housing and Urban Development Department began a grants program to give cities the ability to hire expert contractors to clean up the lead. In 1998 Los Angeles began its own program. Homeowners can qualify to have their property remediated if they meet certain criteria: they must be low-income and have a child age six or under who lives at or regularly visits the property.

Liseth Romero-Martinez, who manages the city's Lead Hazard Remediation Program, created a registry of all the properties that had been successfully remediated of lead since 2007. "I felt that families looking for a lead safe unit could utilize the lead registry," she said.

The average cost per remediation project is about $7,700, said Jose Berumen, who manages the cityâ€™s remediation program. "We try to stay within the cost limits," he said.

Berumen's team assess parts of each housing unit that have lead dangers. The family in the unit is temporarily moved out while a team conducts the work of removing windows or doors, stripping hazardous paint, and repainting.
