Animation: Smoke from the Sand Fire

Produced by Chris Keller & Aaron Mendelson | Updated July 24, 2016

The Sand Fire ignited on the afternoon of July 22 and smoke from the fire quickly spread across the Los Angeles Basin and into Orange County.

Since then, winds have shifted. The image below shows where smoke from the fire could travel, according to United States Forest Service projections. The agency's model, known as the BlueSky Modeling Framework, incorporates information about fires, fuel loadings, consumption, emissions, plume height and smoke trajectory. The image below was pulled from the Blue Sky page on July 24.

The loop begins at 5 p.m. on Sunday, July 24 and ends at 2 p.m. on Wednesday, July 27. The model is only a projection, and the forest service suggests people contact local public health departments for the latest on air quality. The most recent information on the Sand Fire can be found on KPCC's FireTracker.
