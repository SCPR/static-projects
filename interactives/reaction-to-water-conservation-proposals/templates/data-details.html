Search comments regarding state efforts to reduce water use

Produced by Chris Keller | Published April 17, 2015

California water officials received more than 200 letters and emails in response to a proposal that would require local water agencies to cut consumption anywhere from 10 to 35 percent, depending on recent use rates and conservation efforts.

Under the proposal, water agencies in Los Angeles and Long Beach—which have both had ongoing conservation programs and use rates closer to the state average—would be required to cut 20 percent.

Some of those who submitted comments complained they would need more time than allowed to hit their proposed reduction target. Others said the current framework doesn't fully account for past conservation efforts.

The state is expected to release an updated proposal for mandatory cutbacks following a volley of criticism over its original proposal.

These comments were made available by the State Water Resources Control Board.
